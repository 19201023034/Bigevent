# How to Book a Virtual Production Studio — Step by Step

Virtual production sounds complicated — but the process of booking and preparing for a shoot at an LED studio is simpler than you might think. Here is how it works from A to Z.

## Step 1 — Brief and Quote Request

Start with a basic brief: production type (commercial, music video, film, photoshoot), approximate number of days, date or time window, number of actors/models, equipment needs (your own camera, or do you need a rental).

You don't need to know Unreal Engine technical details or LED colour specifications at this stage. A creative vision and a rough schedule are enough.

Send your brief to **info@bigevent.de** — we reply with availability and pricing within 24 hours.

## Step 2 — Studio Recce (Optional but Worth It)

Before your shoot day, visiting the studio is worthwhile. During a recce: you will see the physical dimensions of the screen (10×4 m, P1.9), assess distances between the wall and camera position, discuss lighting setup and positions for the technical crew, test camera movement against LED parallax.

A recce typically takes 1–2 hours and is free when you confirm your booking.

## Step 3 — Content Preparation for the LED Wall

This is the most important creative stage. Content displayed on the LED wall must be prepared at the correct resolution and format:

- Wall resolution: **5120 × 2048 px** (10×4 m, P1.9)
- File format: PNG sequence or video in ProRes 4444 / HAP
- Real-time environment: Unreal Engine 5 (we can run your project or prepare a custom environment)
- Alternative: static backgrounds at 5K resolution

The best results come from environments created specifically for the production — taking into account light direction and colour matching the practical set lighting.

## Step 4 — Shoot Day

The studio is ready 2 hours before your arrival. We provide: calibrated LED wall (colour temperature, brightness, uniformity), LED system operator on-site throughout the day, client preview on a separate monitor, wardrobe and make-up room, secured parking.

You bring: camera crew, camera, talent (actors, models), props.

## Step 5 — Offload and Post-Production

After the shoot, you hand off footage to your post-production team. If you shot in a real-time environment (Unreal Engine), final frames are ready — no rendering required. If you combine LED with VFX elements — an optional side green screen is available.
