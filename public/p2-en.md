# How to Choose an LED Screen for an Exhibition Stand? A Complete Guide

An LED screen at a trade fair stand is today's standard, not a luxury. But choosing the wrong technology or size can mean expensive equipment that makes no impression — or worse, looks worse than the projector slides at the neighbouring stand. Here is what actually matters.

## Pixel Pitch — The Most Important Parameter at a Stand

Trade fairs are an environment where the distance between visitor and screen is small. Guests walk up to the stand, stop 1–3 metres from the screen, speak with your representatives. That means one thing: **P1.9 is the standard for stands**, not a premium option.

At P3.9 from 2 metres you can see individual pixels — the image looks like a grid and text loses sharpness. At P1.9 from the same distance the image is crisp, colours saturated, text readable even at small font sizes.

**Rule of thumb:** minimum comfortable viewing distance (in metres) = pixel pitch value × 1.5.
- P1.9 → comfortable from ~2.8 m
- P3.9 → comfortable from ~5.8 m

At a stand of 20–30 m² with foot traffic 1–4 m from the wall: choose P1.9.

## Screen Size — How to Match It to Your Stand

There is no single answer, but there is a good principle: **the screen should occupy 40–60% of the wall it is mounted on**. Too small and it disappears into the background. Too large and it dominates and disrupts conversation.

| Stand width | Recommended screen |
|-------------|-------------------|
| 3 m | 2 × 1.5 m |
| 4–5 m | 3 × 1.75 m or 4 × 2.5 m |
| 6–8 m | 4 × 2.5 m or 6 × 3.5 m |
| 9–12 m | 6 × 3.5 m or 8 × 4.5 m |

A screen hung centrally on the rear wall works best. If the stand is open on two sides — consider two smaller screens facing different directions.

## Installation — What You Need to Know Before the Crew Arrives

**Installation time:** a 4 × 2.5 m screen requires ~3 hours to install (panels + frame + calibration). Plan delivery for the day before the fair opens, not the morning of opening day.

**Build window:** most trade fair venues (Koelnmesse, Messe Düsseldorf, Messe Frankfurt) assign time slots for installation. Confirm with the organiser when you can access the hall.

**Power supply:** a 10 m² screen consumes ~1.5–2 kW. Make sure your stand has sufficient electrical supply (typically 1 × 16A or 1 × 32A).

**B1 fire certificate:** trade fair venues in Germany require all stand elements — including LED panels and cabling — to hold a B1 fire resistance certificate. Our equipment meets this requirement and we can supply documentation on request.

## Content — Without This Even the Best Screen Won't Work

A P1.9 screen has 512 px per linear metre. A 4 × 2.5 m screen = ~2048 × 1280 px. Your materials must match this resolution — otherwise the image will be blurred or covered in scaling artefacts.

**Formats we accept:** video MP4 (H.264/H.265), PNG sequences, presentation files via HDMI. If you have Full HD (1920 × 1080) content and the screen is a different format — we can advise on adjusting the layout without quality loss.

**Tip:** prepare your video as a seamless loop with no black gaps. A screen that flashes black every few seconds looks like a malfunction.
