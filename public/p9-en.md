# What Size LED Screen to Choose for Your Event? A Practical Guide

One of the most common questions before renting is: "How big a screen do I need?" Too small — guests at the back see nothing. Too large — you overpay and the screen doesn't fit the space. There is a simple formula for this.

## The Golden Rule: Minimum Screen Width Formula

```
Minimum screen width = distance to last viewer ÷ 6
```

If the back row is 18 metres from the screen, the minimum width is **3 metres**. That is the minimum — for comfortable reading of text and graphics, adding 20–30% more is recommended.

## Recommendations by Event Format

### Exhibition Stand (9–20 m² footprint)
Typical screen: **3×1.5 m to 4×2.5 m**
Technology: P1.9 (indoor) — visitors approach closely, pixel pitch matters.
Mounting: freestanding on a frame or integrated into the stand structure.

### Conference Room (50–150 attendees)
Typical screen: **4×2.5 m to 6×3.5 m**
Technology: P1.9 — presentations, charts, video backdrop behind speakers.
Mounting: hung on truss or on legs behind the stage.

### Gala, Congress (200–500 attendees)
Typical screen: **7×4 m to 10×5.5 m**
Technology: P1.9 or P3.9 depending on distance from the stage.
Mounting: flown (suspended) — no poles blocking sightlines.

### Outdoor: Festival, Concert, Public Viewing
Typical screen: **8×4.5 m to 12×6.75 m+**
Technology: P3.9 — IP65, 4,000 nits brightness, fully visible in sunlight.
Mounting: ground support or flown on stage scaffolding.

## Aspect Ratio: 16:9 or Something Else?

Standard video (YouTube, PowerPoint) is **16:9**. Most projects go with this format. If you plan vertical content (e.g. social media ads displayed at a stand) — we can build a 9:16 screen or custom proportions.

## How Many Panels Equal How Many Metres?

Each Absen panel is **500×500 mm** (both P1.9 and P3.9). A 4×2.5 m wall = 8×5 = **40 panels**. An 8×4.5 m wall = 16×9 = **144 panels**.
