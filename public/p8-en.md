# LED Screen vs Projector — Which to Choose for Events, Conferences or Trade Fairs?

A projector is still the first thought for many event organisers. It is familiar, widely available and can seem affordable. But is it still the right choice? We compare both solutions on hard data.

## Brightness: LED Has No Competition Here

A business-class event projector delivers 5,000–20,000 ANSI lumens. That sounds impressive — but lumens are not nits. BigEvent's P3.9 screen delivers **4,000 nits** of brightness across the entire screen surface, regardless of ambient lighting.

In practice: a projector in a room with natural light or outdoors becomes unreadable. An LED screen performs equally well in a dark studio, a daylit conference hall, and in direct sunlight.

| Criterion | LED Screen | Projector |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Brightness in sunlight | ✅ Excellent | ❌ Unreadable |
| Brightness in lit room | ✅ Full | ⚠️ Reduced |
| Contrast ratio | ✅ 8,500:1 | ⚠️ 2,000–5,000:1 |
| Installation time | ✅ 2–4 hours | ⚠️ 4–8 h + calibration |
| Shadow from attendees | ✅ None | ❌ Yes |
| Silent operation | ✅ Yes | ❌ Cooling fan |
| Seamless image | ✅ No frame | ❌ Screen border |

## Shadow — The Projector's Hidden Enemy

Everyone who has presented with a projector knows that moment: the speaker steps toward the screen and blocks half the slide. With an LED screen this problem simply does not exist — the image is emitted by the panels themselves, so a speaker can stand directly in front of the screen.

## When Does a Projector Still Make Sense?

A projector works well when: the room is fully blacked out, the image surface exceeds 20 m² on a tight budget, or the format is non-standard (e.g. projection mapping on a building facade).

For corporate events, trade fairs and standard conference rooms — an LED screen is today more cost-effective to operate, more reliable and looks dramatically better.

## The Real Cost Picture

A projector seems cheaper to hire. But factor in: a projection screen or prepared surface, room blackout solutions (blinds, blackout film), longer technician time for calibration, and the risk of lamp failure. When you add it all up, the price difference is far smaller than it first appears.
