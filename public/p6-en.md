# What is Pixel Pitch and How Does It Affect LED Screen Viewing Distance?

When choosing an LED screen for an exhibition stand, conference, or outdoor concert, you will certainly come across parameters like **P1.9**, **P2.6**, or **P3.9**. The letter \"P\" and its accompanying number represent the **Pixel Pitch**. This is the single most important technical spec that determines screen resolution, close-up image quality, and — crucially — rental price.

What exactly is Pixel Pitch, and how do you choose the right pitch based on the distance your audience will stand from the screen? We explain everything in this simple guide.

## Definition: What is Pixel Pitch?

**Pixel Pitch (dot pitch)** is the distance in millimeters between the centers of two adjacent LED pixels on a display panel.

*   A **P1.9** screen has LEDs spaced **1.9 mm** apart. This translates to an extremely high pixel density (512 pixels per linear meter).
*   A **P3.9** screen has LEDs spaced **3.9 mm** apart. This means a lower pixel density (256 pixels per linear meter), but the panel is much brighter and weather-resistant.

**The rule is simple:** The smaller the pixel pitch, the higher the resolution of the screen for the same physical dimensions, resulting in better close-up image sharpness.

## How to Calculate the Optimal Viewing Distance?

To ensure viewers see a smooth image rather than individual dots (LED diodes), they must stand at a suitable distance from the screen. A simple rule of thumb is used to determine this distance:

> **Minimum Viewing Distance (in meters) = Pixel Pitch (in millimeters)**

This means that:
*   For a **P1.9** screen, the minimum recommended viewing distance is **1.9 meters**.
*   For a **P3.9** screen, the minimum recommended viewing distance is **3.9 meters**.

There is also the concept of **optimal viewing distance**, where the human eye completely stops perceiving individual pixels, and the image becomes perfectly continuous. This is calculated by multiplying the pixel pitch by 2.5:
*   For **P1.9**, the optimal distance is about **4.5 meters**.
*   For **P3.9**, the optimal distance is about **9.7 meters**.

## Decision Guide: P1.9 or P3.9?

### When to choose P1.9 Indoor (Absen NT1.9 V2)?
*   **Small and Medium Exhibition Booths:** Where visitors walk directly past the screen (1-3 meters from the LED wall).
*   **Press Conferences and TV Studios:** Speakers stand close to the screen, and slides with small text or corporate logos must remain crisp for cameras and front rows.
*   **Virtual Production Film Sets:** Cameras record shots close to the screen. High pixel density prevents moiré patterns in camera recordings.

### When to choose P3.9 Outdoor/Indoor (Absen NT3.9W)?
*   **Outdoor Events and Festivals:** The audience stands 5-50 meters away from the stage. The detail of a 1.9 mm pitch is completely lost at this distance, while high brightness (4000 nits) and rain resistance (IP65) are crucial.
*   **Large Island Booths:** Where the main LED screens are rigged high up in the hall ceiling and viewed from 8-15 meters down the aisles.
*   **Large Conference Halls:** Where the screen is 8-12 meters wide, and the front row sits at least 5-6 meters back from the stage.
