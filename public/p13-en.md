# How to Read LED Screen Specs? Brightness, Contrast, Hz and IP Explained

You receive a spec sheet from an AV company and see: "4000 nit, contrast 8500:1, refresh 7680 Hz, IP65." What does it mean in practice? Here is a plain-language glossary of the parameters that actually matter for your event.

## Brightness — Nits (nit / cd/m²)

A **nit** is the unit of screen emission brightness. The more nits, the better the screen performs in bright conditions.

- **800–1500 nit** — indoor, darkened conference rooms, studios
- **2000–3000 nit** — indoor with natural daylight
- **4000+ nit** — outdoor, full sunlight, festivals, public viewing

Our P1.9 panel has **1000 nit** (studio, indoor trade fairs), the P3.9 has **4000 nit** (outdoor). For a conference room with windows, P1.9 is more than sufficient — the image remains sharp in any lighting.

## Contrast — White-to-Black Ratio

A contrast ratio of **8500:1** means white is 8500 times brighter than black. In practice: the higher the contrast, the deeper the blacks and the more saturated the colours. This is especially important at events where you display dark backgrounds with bright graphic elements (logos, text).

Projectors typically achieve 2000–5000:1 — LED screens are clearly superior here, particularly in environments with ambient light.

## Refresh Rate — Hz (Hertz)

**7680 Hz** is the screen's refresh frequency — how many times per second the image is updated. Broadcast TV standard is 50/60 Hz. Why does an LED screen need 7680 Hz?

Video cameras — particularly at fast shutter speeds (1/500, 1/1000 s) — can capture "banding," horizontal dark bars on screen when the screen's refresh frequency is out of sync with the camera shutter. 7680 Hz eliminates this effect completely at any camera setting.

For presentations and livestreams: 3840 Hz is sufficient. For film productions, commercials and broadcast: 7680 Hz is the standard required by all camera operators.

## IP Rating — Protection Against Water and Dust

**IP65** means: "6" — complete protection against dust, "5" — protection against water jets from any direction. In practice: an IP65-rated P3.9 panel can operate during heavy rain, at an open-air festival, without covers, without a tent.

**IP20** (indoor) — no water protection, for indoor use only.

Always check the IP rating if you are planning an outdoor event — even a "covered stage" can have issues with wind-driven rain from the sides.

## Pixel Pitch — Pixel Density

**P1.9** = 1.9 mm between the centres of adjacent pixels = 512 pixels per linear metre = crisp image from 2–3 m viewing distance.
**P3.9** = 3.9 mm = 256 pixels per metre = good image from 5–6 m and beyond.

Rule of thumb: minimum comfortable viewing distance (in metres) ≈ pixel pitch value × 1.5. For P1.9: the human eye cannot distinguish individual pixels from more than 2.8 m away.
